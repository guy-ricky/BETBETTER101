## Match Overview  
On Wednesday, 16 April 2025, the Parc des Princes witnessed a European night to remember as **Paris Saint-Germain defeated Bayern Munich 3–1**, advancing **4–3 on aggregate** to reach the UEFA Champions League semi-finals.  

The tie was finely poised after the **first leg ended 2–1 to Bayern in Munich**, but a relentless PSG side — led by Kylian Mbappé — turned the tables in spectacular fashion.

- **12'** — PSG took the lead through **Ousmane Dembélé**, who capitalised on a loose ball after Neuer spilled Mbappé’s strike.  
- **34'** — **Harry Kane** levelled for Bayern from the penalty spot after a foul on Musiala, restoring their aggregate advantage.

- **77'** — PSG sealed the comeback when **Mbappé** converted from a Hakimi cross, sending the Parc into raptures.  
- **90+3'** — Gianluigi Donnarumma’s stunning save denied Leroy Sané a late equaliser, preserving PSG’s dream.

## Tactical Notes & Analysis  
### Paris Saint-Germain  
- **High pressing & fluid attack:** Luis Enrique’s side suffocated Bayern’s build-up, forcing errors in midfield.  
- **Mbappé’s influence:** Operating freely across the front line, his acceleration and composure decided the tie.  
- **Midfield balance:** Vitinha and Ugarte provided control that PSG lacked in the first leg.  

### Bayern Munich  
- **Conservative approach:** Bayern struggled to match PSG’s tempo and were pinned back for long stretches.  
- **Defensive fragility:** Open spaces behind the full-backs allowed PSG’s wingers to thrive.  
- **Missed chances:** Musiala and Sané both went close, but Bayern’s finishing lacked conviction.

---

## Comparing the Legs  
- **First Leg (Munich):** Bayern 2–1 PSG — Goals from Kane and Sané gave the Bavarians a slender lead, while Mbappé’s late strike offered PSG a vital away goal.  
- **Second Leg (Paris):** PSG 3–1 Bayern — A complete turnaround, with PSG exploiting home advantage and improved defensive structure.  

**Aggregate: PSG 4–3 Bayern** — The French champions advanced, avenging last season’s quarter-final exit to the same opponents.
